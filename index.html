Intro

I’ve always enjoyed connecting with people, and over the years, I’ve been fortunate to build friendships across the world — from Bangladesh to Japan, and now in the United States. Moving to the U.S. was a big leap. I came here alone, knowing no one on the entire continent, and the cultural shift was intense. But with time, I adapted and built deep, meaningful relationships that I truly value.

I think the transition wasn’t overwhelming because I’ve developed a strong ability to connect with people — not just talk to them. That connection has helped me grow personally and professionally, especially in team environments where I thrive. Soccer played a huge role in shaping who I am. It gave me purpose, discipline, and direction at a time when I didn’t have much of that. It’s also been a big part of my 60-pound weight loss journey, which I share on my YouTube channel which is linked on the main page on this website too. Work

I recently earned my CompTIA Security+ certification — a big milestone in my cybersecurity journey. At first, I thought it would be all memorization and difficult concepts, based on what I’d heard. But once I started studying, I realized I actually enjoyed the process.

What made the biggest difference was approaching the certification as a chance to learn, not just pass. That shift helped me see how the different topics connect — from threat analysis to network security and risk management. It was eye-opening to see how all the pieces work together in the real world. Understanding those relationships made the experience not just rewarding, but exciting. It deepened my passion for cybersecurity and made me even more curious about how systems are secured from end to end.

Alongside my studies, I worked with the Housing IT Services team, where I provided tech support to both staff and residents.

From troubleshooting hardware to managing network access, this hands-on experience gave me a practical perspective on the technical issues people face every day. It also taught me the importance of clear communication and empathy when solving tech problems — something that complements the technical skills I’ve built through my certifications.

My time there made a real impact, and I was proud to receive a letter of recommendation highlighting my dedication, problem-solving abilities, and teamwork.
